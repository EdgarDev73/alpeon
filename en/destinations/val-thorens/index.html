Why Val Thorens delivers
the most reliable rental income in the Alps

Val Thorens is the highest ski resort in Europe — and that altitude is not merely a statistic. It translates directly into rental performance. At 2,300m, the resort guarantees snow conditions from mid-November through early May, eliminating the weather risk that undermines income at lower-altitude destinations.

The longest season in France

Val Thorens operates 22 weeks per year — more than any other French ski resort. This extended season means more bookable weeks, more guaranteed rent weeks, and a higher annual yield relative to the property's value. Owners who have properties at multiple altitudes consistently report that Val Thorens outperforms on total annual revenue.

A performance-first international clientele

Guests come to Val Thorens for one reason: maximising ski time on 600km of the best high-altitude terrain in Europe. British, Scandinavian and international ski performance enthusiasts are the core market — guests who book early, stay the full week and return annually. Slope-side apartments command a 20–35% premium over non-piste-access equivalents.

Access to the 3 Vallées mega-domain

Val Thorens is the highest gateway to the 3 Vallées — the largest linked ski area in the world. Guests staying in Val Thorens access Méribel, Courchevel and the full 600km network. This mega-domain access is a powerful marketing asset that fills calendars and justifies premium weekly rates.

Frequently asked questions

Everything you need to know
about property management in Val Thorens

Why does Val Thorens have the longest ski season in France?
At 2,300m altitude — the highest ski resort in Europe — Val Thorens benefits from guaranteed natural snow and cold temperatures from mid-November through early May. This 22-week season is the longest of any French ski resort and is unaffected by the warm spells that reduce lower-altitude resort seasons. For rental property owners, this translates to more bookable weeks and a higher annual yield.
How does guaranteed rent work in Val Thorens?
ALPÉON signs a yearly contract with you and becomes the direct tenant of your Val Thorens property. You receive a fixed monthly rent throughout the contracted season, regardless of actual occupancy rates, weather conditions or cancellations. The rent is determined based on a thorough market assessment of your property before the season begins.
What weekly rates does an apartment in Val Thorens command?
Val Thorens weekly rates range from €3,000 for a compact studio in a standard week, to €20,000 for a large premium slope-side apartment during Christmas or February half-term. Slope-side access commands a premium of 20–35% over non-piste equivalents. ALPÉON's dynamic pricing model maximises revenue across all 22 weeks of the season.
What are the tax implications for UK owners?
Rental income from your Val Thorens property is taxed in France under the LMNP furnished letting regime. UK owners benefit from the France-UK double taxation convention. The micro-BIC regime (50% or 71% for classified properties) or the actual expense regime are both available. We recommend a specialist alpine tax adviser.
Is slope-side access important for rental income in Val Thorens?
Yes — in Val Thorens, ski-in/ski-out or slope-side access commands a significant premium of 20–35% over equivalent properties without direct piste access. Our pricing model accounts precisely for this premium and we actively market slope-side access as a key selling point to international ski guests who prioritise it above all other property features.
Does ALPÉON manage properties in Les Menuires and Saint-Martin-de-Belleville?
Yes. ALPÉON manages properties across the entire Belleville valley: Val Thorens (highest and longest season), Les Menuires (excellent family-friendly value, same ski domain) and Saint-Martin-de-Belleville (beautiful traditional Savoyard village with authentic character and growing international appeal). Each village attracts a distinct profile of guests and commands different rental dynamics.
How does ALPÉON market Val Thorens properties to international guests?
ALPÉON distributes across 20+ platforms including Airbnb, Booking.com, VRBO and specialist ski rental platforms, alongside 50+ luxury agency partnerships. For Val Thorens, we specifically target the international ski performance market — British, Scandinavian and Eastern European guests who prioritise guaranteed snow conditions and maximum vertical drop. Our multilingual listings and partnerships with specialist ski travel agencies ensure maximum reach in these markets.
