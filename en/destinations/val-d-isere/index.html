The market

Val d'Isère: the world's most iconic expert ski resort

A playground for elite skiers and a magnet for affluent international visitors, Val d'Isère consistently commands some of the highest rental rates in the Alps.

World-class skiing, world-class demand

Val d'Isère, together with Tignes in the Espace Killy domain, offers 300 km of pistes stretching to 3,456 m. The resort's legendary black runs — including the famous Bellevarde face used for Olympic downhills — attract the world's most discerning skiers. This status translates directly into exceptional rental demand: properties in Val d'Isère achieve the highest nightly rates per square metre of any resort in the French Alps.

British & Scandinavian heartland

Over 55% of Val d'Isère's visitors are international, with British guests forming the single largest group. The resort has been a British holiday staple since the 1960s, and today it draws an affluent clientele willing to pay premium rates for the right property. Scandinavian and American visitors add further depth to the demand pool during mid-season weeks that might otherwise soften at lesser resorts.

Year-round investment appeal

Val d'Isère's high altitude (1,850 m resort base, 3,456 m top) ensures reliable snow from late November through early May. The resort's summer hiking and mountain biking offer emerging off-season potential. Property values have appreciated by over 30% in the past five years, reinforcing Val d'Isère's position as one of the most compelling ski property investment locations in Europe.

Questions & answers

Frequently asked questions

How does the guaranteed rent work in Val d'Isère?

ALPÉON signs a multi-year commercial lease directly with you and pays a fixed monthly rent regardless of actual occupancy. You receive your income whether the property is occupied or not — no management fees, no empty-week deductions.

What rental yield can I expect in Val d'Isère?

Net yields typically range from 4% to 7% of property value per year, depending on property size, location within the resort, and quality of furnishings. Val d'Isère properties command some of the highest absolute rent figures in the French Alps.

I'm a UK resident — how is rental income taxed?

Income from French property is taxed in France under the Franco-British double tax treaty. UK residents declare the income in both countries but receive a tax credit in the UK to avoid double taxation. We recommend consulting a cross-border tax adviser who specialises in French property.

Which villages in Val d'Isère does ALPÉON cover?

We manage properties across Val d'Isère Village (the main resort centre), Le Fornet (traditional hamlet, quieter), La Daille (ski-in/ski-out gondola access), and Les Carats (luxury residences). Each micro-area commands different rates and attracts a distinct clientele.

What weekly rates does my property achieve?

Weekly rental rates in Val d'Isère range from approximately €5,000 for a studio or one-bed to over €40,000 for a luxury chalet during Christmas or February half-term. ALPÉON's international marketing and tour operator partnerships consistently achieve above-market rates.

How does ALPÉON attract British and Scandinavian guests?

We partner with premium UK tour operators (Powder White, Scott Dunn, and others), list on international platforms, and operate dedicated English-language campaigns targeting the affluent British and Scandinavian skier demographic that represents over 55% of Val d'Isère's international visitors.

How quickly can I start receiving guaranteed rent?

Once we visit your property and agree on the guaranteed rent amount, the lease can be signed within 2 weeks. You typically receive your first payment within 30 days of signing the contract.
