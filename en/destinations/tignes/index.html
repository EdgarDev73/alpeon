Tignes: France's longest ski season

With a glacier at 3,456 m and a base altitude of 2,100 m at Val Claret, Tignes offers the longest guaranteed ski season in France — opening in late October and closing in early May.

Glacier skiing = 26 weeks of potential rental income

Tignes' Grande Motte glacier operates almost year-round, and the resort itself typically runs for 26 weeks — compared to 18–20 weeks at lower altitude resorts. For property investors, this extended season means more weeks of rental income and a longer window for ALPÉON's guaranteed rent to compound. No other French resort can match Tignes' combination of reliable natural snow, glacier access, and season length.

Adventurous international clientele

Tignes attracts a specific type of guest: serious skiers and snowboarders who choose the resort for its glacier, challenging off-piste terrain, and overall snow reliability. British, Scandinavian, Northern European, and increasingly Eastern European visitors dominate the clientele. This demographic is typically younger, more adventurous, and willing to book early for peak weeks — creating a deep and reliable demand pool that ALPÉON taps through specialist international partnerships.

Exceptional value per square metre

Tignes properties offer significantly better value than equivalent properties in Val d'Isère, which shares the same 300 km Espace Killy ski domain. A three-bedroom apartment in Tignes Val Claret may cost 30–40% less to acquire than a comparable property in Val d'Isère, while accessing identical terrain. This makes Tignes one of the most compelling risk-adjusted ski property investment opportunities in the Alps — strong rental yields supported by a highly international demand base and the longest season in France.

Questions & answers

Frequently asked questions

How does the guaranteed rent work in Tignes?

ALPÉON signs a multi-year commercial lease directly with you and pays a fixed monthly rent regardless of actual occupancy. You receive your income whether the property is occupied or not — including the extended glacier season from October to May.

What makes Tignes' season longer than other resorts?

Tignes has a year-round glacier (Grande Motte, 3,456 m) and a high base altitude of 2,100 m at Val Claret. This combination gives Tignes a typical season running from late October to early May — 26 weeks, the longest of any French ski resort and 6–8 weeks longer than resorts below 1,800 m.

What rental yield can I expect in Tignes?

Net yields typically range from 3.5% to 6% of property value per year. Tignes offers excellent value per square metre compared to Val d'Isère while accessing the same 300 km Espace Killy terrain, making it one of the best risk-adjusted ski property investments in the Alps.

I'm a UK resident — how is rental income from Tignes taxed?

Income from French property is taxed in France under the Franco-British double tax treaty. UK residents receive a tax credit to avoid double taxation. We recommend consulting a cross-border tax specialist who understands both French and UK property tax regimes.

Which villages in Tignes does ALPÉON cover?

We manage properties across Tignes Val Claret (2,100 m, highest and most lively), Tignes Le Lac (1,850 m, central with lake views), Les Brévières (traditional Savoyard village, lower but charming), and Tignes Les Boisses. Each village commands different rates and attracts a distinct clientele.

Who are Tignes' main visitors?

Tignes attracts serious skiers and snowboarders who choose the resort for its glacier, challenging off-piste terrain, and snow reliability. British and Scandinavian guests form the largest international groups, with Northern European and Eastern European visitors growing year on year. The demographic skews slightly younger and more adventure-focused than Courchevel or Méribel.

How quickly can I start receiving guaranteed rent?

Once we visit your property and agree on the guaranteed rent amount, the lease can be signed within 2 weeks and your first payment received within 30 days of signing the contract.
